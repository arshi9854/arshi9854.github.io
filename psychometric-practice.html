Thomas International Assessment — Practice

Canonical uses two Thomas tools: the GIA (cognitive aptitude) and PPA (DISC behavioral profile). Practice all sections here before your real assessment.

GIA — 5 subtests (~25 min total)
• Reasoning — Word analogies and logical relationships
• Perceptual Speed — Spot identical string pairs quickly (speed test)
• Number Speed & Accuracy — Fast arithmetic, sequences, percentages
• Word Meaning — Synonyms, antonyms, vocabulary
• Spatial Visualisation — Which option shows the shape rotated (not mirrored)?

PPA / DISC — Behavioral Profile (~8 min)
• 24 groups of 4 adjectives — pick MOST and LEAST like you at work
• No right or wrong answers — measures D (Dominance), I (Influence), S (Steadiness), C (Compliance)

• GIA is strictly timed — speed AND accuracy matter

• For perceptual: scan character by character, don't try to "see" the whole string at once
• DISC: answer instinctively for your work self, don't overthink

Strategy & Tips

Based on real candidate experience with the Thomas GIA and PPA.

⚡ GIA — General Strategy

  • Speed over perfection — blank = 0, wrong = 0 or -1. Always guess rather than leave blank.
  • Each subtest is timed separately — you cannot go back.
  • Practice mental arithmetic daily: 17×8, 144÷12, 15% of 240, etc.
  • Read the question fully before looking at options — analogies especially.

🔷 Spatial — Mental Rotation Tips

  • Pick one distinctive feature of the shape (a corner, a notch, the short end) and track where it moves when rotated.
  • Eliminate mirror images first — they look right but are flipped.
  • Rotations preserve the shape's "handedness" — mirrors reverse it.
  • Practice: hold a pen, rotate it 90° clockwise — the tip moves from pointing right to pointing down.

👁️ Perceptual Speed — Scanning Tips

  • Scan character by character left to right — don't try to "see" the whole string at once.
  • Common tricks: lowercase/uppercase swap (l vs L), similar digits (6 vs 9, 0 vs O), symbol swap (# vs $).
  • In the real test you have ~30 items in ~3 minutes — about 6 seconds each. Train yourself to go faster.

🔢 Numerical — Speed Arithmetic Tips

  • Know your multiplication tables up to 12×12 cold.
  • For percentages: 10% = move decimal left. 15% = 10% + 5%. 25% = ÷4.
  • For sequences: check +, ×, ÷, and Fibonacci patterns first.
  • For word problems: write down the formula before plugging numbers in.

🧩 DISC — Behavioral Profile Tips

  • Answer for your work self — not who you wish you were, not your personal life.
  • Don't try to game it — the PPA has 24 groups with built-in consistency checks.
  • Canonical values: autonomy, written communication, quality, remote-first discipline. High C (systematic) and S (reliable) align well — but be honest.
  • Real test: 24 groups, ~8 min total = ~20 seconds per group max.

📋 Day-of Checklist

  • Stable internet, quiet room, laptop preferred over phone.
  • Do the GIA in the morning when your mind is sharpest.
  • Keep paper and pen for numerical scratch work.
  • Complete DISC in one sitting — pausing mid-way can affect consistency scoring.
