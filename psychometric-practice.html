What is the Thomas Assessment?

Canonical uses two Thomas tools: the GIA (General Intelligence Assessment) and the PPA (Personal Profile Analysis / DISC). There are no right or wrong answers in the DISC — it measures your behavioral style. The GIA is timed and does have correct answers.

GIA: ~25 minutes DISC: ~8 minutes 5 GIA subtests No right/wrong in DISC
GIA — General Intelligence Assessment

The GIA measures cognitive speed and accuracy across 5 subtests. You must work as fast and accurately as possible. Each subtest is strictly timed (2–4 minutes each).

1. Reasoning — Word/logical analogies (e.g., "Cat is to Kitten as Dog is to ___")
2. Perceptual Speed — Spot identical pairs in a grid of letters/symbols quickly
3. Number Speed & Accuracy — Fast arithmetic and number comparisons
4. Word Meaning — Vocabulary: synonyms, antonyms
5. Spatial Visualisation — Mental rotation, shape matching

DISC — Personal Profile Analysis (PPA)

Shows 24 groups of 4 adjectives. For each group, select the word most like you and the word least like you in a work context. Be honest and fast — don't overthink.
Key advice from Canonical candidates: The GIA score matters more than DISC for technical roles. Speed is crucial — don't spend too long on any single question. Mark it and move on. For DISC, answer as you are at work, not as you wish you were.

DISC — Personal Profile Analysis (PPA)

For each group of 4 words, select MOST like you and LEAST like you in a work context. Answer instinctively. There are no right or wrong answers — this maps your behavioral style. Canonical uses this to understand how you work, not to filter you out.

D = Dominance (results-driven) I = Influence (people-oriented) S = Steadiness (reliable, calm) C = Compliance (accurate, systematic)
For a software engineering role at Canonical: Canonical values autonomy, remote-first discipline, written communication, and quality. High C (compliance/accuracy) and S (steadiness/reliability) typically align well with their culture. Being high D (decisive, results-focused) is also valued. There's no "right" profile — just be authentic.

Strategy Guide — How to Ace the Thomas Assessment

Based on real candidate feedback from Canonical applicants and Thomas assessment documentation.

GIA — General Strategy
✅ Speed over perfection — The GIA measures speed AND accuracy. A blank answer scores 0; a wrong answer scores -1 (in some versions). Move on quickly if stuck.
✅ Practice mental arithmetic daily — 12×7, 144÷12, 17+38, etc. The numerical section trips people up on simple math under time pressure.
✅ Analogies pattern: Always identify the relationship first. "Kitten is to Cat as Lamb is to ___" → young to adult → Sheep.
✅ Perceptual speed: Scan character by character left to right. Don't try to "see" the whole string at once.
✅ Spatial: Pick a distinctive feature (a corner, a notch) and track where it goes when rotated. Eliminate mirror images first.
DISC — Behavioral Profile Strategy
✅ Answer for work, not life — Think about how you behave at your job, not at home.
✅ Don't try to game it — The PPA has built-in consistency checks. Inconsistent answers are flagged. Just be honest.
✅ Most = your natural strength, Least = what drains you
✅ For Canonical specifically: They work remote-first, value written communication, trust people to self-manage. If that's genuinely you, it'll show.
✅ Time yourself: Real DISC takes ~8 minutes for 24 groups. That's 20 seconds per group max.
Day-of Checklist

DISC Dimensions Explained
D — Dominance: Direct, decisive, results-oriented, competitive. Words: assertive, bold, demanding, forceful, strong-willed.

I — Influence: Enthusiastic, optimistic, social, persuasive. Words: sociable, inspiring, charming, expressive, lively.

S — Steadiness: Patient, reliable, calm, supportive, consistent. Words: calm, patient, methodical, sincere, team-player.

C — Compliance/Conscientiousness: Accurate, analytical, systematic, quality-focused. Words: precise, careful, diplomatic, systematic, fact-oriented.
